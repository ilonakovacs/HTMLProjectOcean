About the Midnight Zone


The Bathypelagic Zone, or Midnight Zone, is a subzone of the deepest zone in the ocean: the Abyssopelagic Zone. This subzone comes 'above' the abyss. The depth ranges between 3,300-13,100 feet and can cause high pressures around 5,850 pounds per square inch. Because no sunlight can reach this subzone (hense the name), the waters tempurature is close to freezing at about 39Â°F.

This subzone has no plants or phytoplankton due to its lack of sunlight. The aquatic animals that live in here are often scavengers or predators who help themselves to a vast variety of meal options due to scarcity. The darkness also provides a sort of safety, as the creatures often lack camouflage or bright colors. Some of them are even blind!
